About This Book

A strategic guide to the agentic enterprise

About the Author

Jeff Schneider

Jeff Schneider is the founder and former CEO of MomentumSI, which he led to a successful exit to VMware. At VMware, he held leadership positions on the cloud teams, helping shape enterprise cloud strategy during a pivotal era of digital transformation. His experience building and scaling technology companies, combined with his front-row seat to the AI revolution, informs his perspective on how organizations must adapt to thrive in the agentic era.

The Premise

The transformation happening right now is not about offloading work to AI so your people can focus on "higher-value" activities. That is still thinking like a human-centered organization. The real shift is this: You are removing humans from the critical path of enterprise execution.

This book provides a comprehensive framework for understanding, implementing, and thriving in the age of AI agents. From creating your first agents to managing enterprise-wide transformation, it covers the strategic, operational, and human dimensions of the agentic revolution.

What You'll Learn

Across 16 chapters organized in five parts, this book explores:

  • Why offloading to AI agents is an existential imperative, not just an efficiency play
  • How to build, deploy, and manage AI agents at every level of autonomy
  • Strategies for enterprise transformation and organizational change
  • The human side: new roles, skills, and the uncomfortable truths about workforce impact
  • Governance, ethics, and the boardroom implications of agentic operations
